Tribute Page - Charlie Chaplin

Sir Charles Spencer Chaplin

An English comic actor, filmmaker, and composer who rose to fame in the era of silent film.

Here's a time line of Chaplin's life:

  • 1889 - Born in Walworth, London, England
  • 1899-1900 - Chaplin became a member of the Eight Lancashire Lads clog-dancing troupe, with whom he toured English music halls with.
  • 1905 - At 16 years old, Chaplin starred in the play's West End production at the Duke of York's Theatre.
  • 1906
    • He completed one final tour of Sherlock Holmes.
    • Chaplin joined the juvenile act Casey's Circus, where he developed popular burlesque pieces and was soon the star of the show.
    • Chaplin had joined Fred Karno's prestigious comedy company.
  • 1910 - He was given the lead in a new sketch, Jimmy the Fearless. It was a big success, and Chaplin received considerable press attention.
  • 1914
    • Chaplin began working for the Keystone studio.
    • The one-reeler Making a Living marked his film acting debut and was released.
    • The film was Mabel's Strange Predicament, but "the Tramp" character, as it became known, debuted to audiences in Kid Auto Races at Venice – shot later than Mabel's Strange Predicament.
    • Caught in the Rain, was Chaplin's directorial debut and was highly successful.
    • He had a supporting role in the first feature length comedy film, Tillie's Punctured Romance, directed by Sennett and starring Marie Dressler, which was a commercial success and increased his popularity.
  • 1914-1917 - Chaplin met Edna Purviance. The pair also formed a romantic relationship
  • 1915 - Chaplin became a cultural phenomenon. Shops were stocked with Chaplin merchandise, he was featured in cartoons and comic strips, and several songs were written about him.
  • 1916 - Behind the Screen and The Rink completed Chaplin's releases
  • 1917
    • He made only four more films for Mutual: Easy Street, The Cure, The Immigrant, and The Adventurer.
    • Chaplin signed to complete eight films for First National Exhibitors' Circuit in return for $1 million ($20 million today).
  • 1918 - A Dog's Life, was the first film under the new contract.
  • 1922
    • He worked to fulfil his First National contract, releasing Pay Day.
    • He began filming A Woman of Paris, a romantic drama about ill-fated lovers.
  • 1936 - After recording the music, Chaplin released Modern Times.
  • 1946 - He finally began filming a project that had been in development. Monsieur Verdoux was a black comedy, the story of a French bank clerk, Verdoux (Chaplin), who loses his job and begins marrying and murdering wealthy widows to support his family.
  • 1954 - He began developing his first European film, A King in New York.
  • 1963 - The Plaza Theater in New York started a year-long series of Chaplin's films, including Monsieur Verdoux and Limelight, which gained excellent reviews from American critics.
  • 1967 - Chaplin began work on A Countess from Hong Kong, a romantic comedy based on a script he had written for Paulette Goddard in the 1930s.
  • 1971 - He was made a Commander of the National Order of the Legion of Honour at the Cannes Film Festival.
  • 1972 - The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences offered Chaplin an Honorary Award, which Robinson sees as a sign that America "wanted to make amends".
  • 1975 - Chaplin was awarded a knighthood by Queen Elizabeth II, though he was too weak to kneel and received the honour in his wheelchair.
  • 1977 - Chaplin died at home after suffering a stroke in his sleep. He was 88 years old.
